Help Build the DTI Board

Shape the Future of Linux Transparency

The Distro Transparency Index (DTI) is seeking a small but committed board to support the review and evolution of its evaluation framework for the 2025/2026 cycle. This is an open and voluntary initiative with no affiliation to commercial or governmental entities.

Board Composition

We aim to form a board of 3 to 5 independent members. At a minimum, the following figures are sought:

  • Linux distribution analyst or long-time user – familiar with community dynamics and open source tools.
  • Open governance or transparency advocate – someone committed to documentation, fairness, and openness.
  • Independent reviewer / observer – to validate methodologies and ensure impartiality.

Optional profiles may include contributors to FOSS communities, educators, writers, or legal/ethical advisors in open source contexts.

What We Expect

  • Interest in open source and transparency.
  • No active affiliation with specific distributions (to ensure neutrality).
  • Willingness to review documentation and discuss evaluation criteria (approx. 2–3 hours/month).
  • Clear ethical conduct and collaborative spirit.
